How to use this guide?

To make the most out of this guide, it is important for you to take your time to think about what you want to search for or find, what you need, and, if possible, verbalise or write it down. This can help you to keep your focus and make your search more effective.

What we present here are just some ideas for using the guide effectively. However, feel free to follow our recommendations or navigate at your own path and therefore become an autonomous user. There are different ways to access the content of the guide:

  • using the fixed menu at the top left-hand corner in the tab ‘Structure’
  • scrolling through the different sections and sub-sections of the branch-like image
  • using the search box on the top of the screen.
SOME IDEAS FOR USING THIS GUIDE
  • If your goal is simply to know the contents of the guide,you can browse any of the sections and subsections. You will find short explanations as well as links to resources of all kinds (videos, podcasts, websites, articles, etc.) that expand on this information.
  • If you prefer to consult a specific content (e.g. some active methodologies using digital media, or online training courses to complete your training) you can expand the corresponding branch in the image or access it from the fixed menu in the ‘Structure’ tab.
  • If you need to plan a language lesson using digital technologies in an effective way, we recommend you to follow the following steps within the guide:
    • 1. Select the teaching modality that suits your educational context or that you would like to implement.
    • 2. Select the methodology you would like to apply.
    • 3. Select the linguistic competence you would like to work on in your class.
    • 4. Choose a learning strategy you would like your students to activate and the digital tool that could be applied.
    • 5. Design the planning for your classes. In the Design course section you will find an example of a planning design adapted to all teaching modalities.
    • 6. Design your course. In the Design course section you will find an example of a French learning scenario designed with the flipped classroom methodology.
  • You can also use this guide to be updated with the latest developments and research in digital pedagogies or the most recent applications of digital resources. In this case, you may want to consult the DIAL4U dynamic library.

REMEMBER: Feel free to use this guide at your convenience and according to your needs and interests. Enjoy the DIAL4U interactive guide experience!
